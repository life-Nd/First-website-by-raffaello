Michelangelo Buonarroti

A Renaissance Master

Early Life and Training

Born in Caprese, Italy, Michelangelo showed artistic talent from a young age. He apprenticed under Domenico Ghirlandaio and later studied sculpture in the Medici gardens.

Sculptural Masterpieces

Michelangelo's sculptures, such as David and Pietà, are renowned for their anatomical accuracy and emotional depth. These works established him as a leading sculptor of his time.

The Sistine Chapel

Commissioned by Pope Julius II, Michelangelo painted the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel, a monumental work depicting scenes from the Book of Genesis. This project cemented his legacy as one of history's greatest artists.

Architectural Contributions

Michelangelo also made significant contributions to architecture, including his design for the dome of St. Peter's Basilica in Vatican City.

Later Life and Legacy

Michelangelo continued to work and create until his death in 1564. His influence on Western art is immeasurable, and his works continue to inspire awe and admiration.
